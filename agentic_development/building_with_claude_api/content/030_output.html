# One-Day Marathon Training Meal Plan

Here is a one-day meal plan for an athlete training for a marathon with optimized carbohydrate timing around a 2-hour morning run:

* **Calorie Target:** Approximately 3,200 calories
* **Macronutrient Breakdown:** Protein (120g), Fat (80g), Carbs (480g)

**Meal Plan:**

* **Pre-Run Snack (5:30 AM - 30 minutes before 6:00 AM run):** Banana (120g) with honey (20g) and white bread (40g).
  * Protein: 4g, Fat: 1g, Carbs: 55g

* **Post-Run Recovery (8:15 AM - within 30 minutes of finishing):** Chocolate milk (500g) with a bagel (90g) and almond butter (20g).
  * Protein: 20g, Fat: 10g, Carbs: 90g

* **Breakfast (9:30 AM):** Oatmeal (100g dry weight) cooked with whole milk (240g), blueberries (150g), and granola (40g). Orange juice (250ml).
  * Protein: 18g, Fat: 12g, Carbs: 95g

* **Mid-Morning Snack (12:00 PM):** Rice cakes (50g) with peanut butter (30g) and banana (100g).
  * Protein: 12g, Fat: 16g, Carbs: 55g

* **Lunch (1:30 PM):** Grilled chicken breast (140g), jasmine rice (180g cooked), steamed carrots (150g), and olive oil drizzle (10ml).
  * Protein: 38g, Fat: 12g, Carbs: 75g

* **Afternoon Snack (4:00 PM):** Greek yogurt (200g) with honey (15g), granola (30g), and strawberries (100g).
  * Protein: 18g, Fat: 8g, Carbs: 60g

* **Dinner (6:30 PM):** Baked salmon (130g), sweet potato (250g), steamed broccoli (200g), and sesame oil (10ml).
  * Protein: 28g, Fat: 15g, Carbs: 75g

* **Evening Hydration (8:00 PM):** Sports drink with electrolytes (500ml) and a handful of pretzels (30g).
  * Protein: 2g, Fat: 1g, Carbs: 40g

**Hydration Notes:** Drink water throughout the day (at least 3-4 liters), with electrolyte-enhanced beverages during and after the morning run to support recovery and maintain electrolyte balance for endurance training.

This meal plan prioritizes easily digestible carbohydrates strategically timed around your training run, supports muscle recovery with adequate protein, and maintains proper hydration for marathon preparation.
